Cuba Libre

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 ounce freshly squeezed lime juice, from 1/2 lime
  • 2 ounces light rum
  • 4 ounces cola

How to:

  1. Squeeze the lime into a longdrink glass.
  2. Pour the rum and add the cola.
  3. Add the garnish
  4. Ready to serve, as easy as it gets!

Fun Fact:

As the story goes, in the year 1900, a captain in the U.S. Army stationed in Havana during the Spanish-American War poured Coca-Cola and a squeeze of lime into his Bacardí and toasted his Cuban comrades by calling out in the bar, “Por Cuba Libre!” (“To a free Cuba!”)

Its origin is associated with the heavy U.S. presence in Cuba following the Spanish–American War of 1898; the drink's traditional name, "Cuba libre" (Free Cuba), was the slogan of the Cuban independence movement. The Cuba libre is sometimes said to have been created during the Spanish–American War.

Perfect for:

If you are having a summer party and want something simple but delicious to serve the Cuba Libre, with its three ingredients, is perfect!
