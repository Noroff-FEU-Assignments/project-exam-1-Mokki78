Caramel Apple Hot Toddy

Ingredients:

  • Apple cider
  • Caramel Vodka
  • Bourbon (optional but highly recommended!)
  • Cinnamon Sticks
  • Whipped Cream (to top it off)

How to:

  1. Combine apple cider, vodka, bourbon, and cinnamon sticks in a pot.
  2. Heat on the stove top and let it simmer until hot.
  3. Pour into heat-proof mugs or glasses and top with whipped cream!

We used cool whip to top it off, but real whipped cream would be just as good (if not better). I prefer this drink served hot, but it would also be good served chilled, or even frozen into a slush! This recipe makes about 12 glasses, but you can easily double the recipe if you have guests over, or if you are throwing a holiday party.

Fun Fact:

It is believed that the hot toddy was invented to disguise raw Scotch, which in the 18th century was unpalatable

The name Hot toddy originally started in India (controlled by the British at the time) in the 1610s and was derived from the Hindi word “taddy,” which translated into a drink made with fermented palm sap. Fast forward to 1786, where the word became official and was defined as an alcoholic drink made with hot water, spices, and sugar.

Perfect for:

This is the perfect winter and Christmas drink. Surprise your mum or dad, sister or boy/girlfriend with this warm and delicious treat after a long day out in the cold. It is also a perfect dessert after a big meal.
